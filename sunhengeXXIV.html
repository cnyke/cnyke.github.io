Sunhenge XXIV

June 8, 2024 | Claire Nyquist

Basking in the light of our local star, life giver
Swirling in the shadow of Jupiter, asteroid diverter
Shielded by the geomagnetic field, solar wind deflector
All of these quirks of our beloved Earth allow a planet full of life
Contrast with all the forever unalive orbs nearby
The barren deserts and dead volcanoes of Mars
The swirling clouds of Venus
The cold far reaches of Neptune
Contrast with the blossoming of leaves in Spring
Lush jungles
Fragrant lilacs and putrid corpse flowers
Spiny cacti and slinky lizards
Wild panthers and eight billion humans
Oh what a world
Oh what a wonder
We'll spend our whole lives trying to fathom
The awe warranted by our own unlikely existence
